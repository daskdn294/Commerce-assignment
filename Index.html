Task 2 Written Explanation

Interdependence with the Producer Sector

Our household interacts with the producer sector in two completely different ways, showing we depend on each other:


  • As Employees (NZ Police): My parent works for the NZ Police. The real flow here is the hard work and skills they give to the job, like understanding criminal law, managing multiple stations, and running district operations during big events. In exchange, the money flow is the salary the NZ Police pays to our household so we can survive. The police needs our labor to achieve organisation goals, and we need their money!
  • As Consumers (Woolworths, Pak n Save, BP): We rely on supermarkets and shops for things we need every week. The money flow goes from our household using credit cards or online banking to pay them. The real flow is the actual groceries, petrol, and broadband internet (from 2degrees) they give back to us. The firms need our spending to make a profit, and we need their goods.

Interdependence with Other Sectors

Here is how our household depends on the Financial and Government sectors:


  • Financial Sector (ASB Bank): We use ASB bank as our main financial institution for storing our money safely and getting a mortgage. The money flow from us to them is the fees and interest payments on the mortgage. The real flow is the safety they provide because of banking regulations, plus financial advice and the loan itself. They rely on us putting salary into the bank, and we rely on them for buying our house.
  • Government Sector: The money flow from our household is income tax deducted straight from my parents salaries. The real flow we get back from the government is public services like schooling, roads, and healthcare. The Auckland City Council also provides rubbish and water services using these funds. The government needs our taxes to run the country, and we need their services to live safely.
